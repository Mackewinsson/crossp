A vague contract is how good projects turn into disputes. You do not need to be a lawyer to demand clarity.

## Non-negotiables

- **IP assignment:** source code you pay for is yours.
- **Accounts:** cloud, domains, and repos under your organization.
- **Deliverables:** what “done” means for each phase or sprint.
- **Access:** continuous repository and staging access.
- **Confidentiality** and data handling for customer information.

## Delivery and money

- Sprint or milestone cadence with demo expectations.
- Payment tied to accepted work, not only calendar time.
- Change-control process that does not reset the whole deal.
- Exit clause: what you keep if you pause or stop.

## Quality clauses worth asking for

- Tests for critical paths included by default.
- Documentation and handover checklist on exit.
- Named lead / escalation contact.

ConaiSoft contracts around clear sprints, ownership that stays with you, and demos you can accept or reject — ask for the same standard everywhere.
