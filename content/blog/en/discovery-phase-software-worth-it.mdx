Buyers ask whether discovery is worth paying for. The answer depends on what you get back.

## Discovery is worth it when it produces

- A prioritized MVP slice.
- Technical risk list (integrations, data, AI, compliance).
- Architecture options with trade-offs.
- A sprint plan and budget band you can fund.

## Discovery is not worth it when it only produces

- Generic personas and sticky-note workshops.
- Beautiful decks with no repository or prototype path.
- A sales exercise to justify a huge fixed quote.

## Timeboxes that stay honest

Many focused discoveries fit in days to a couple of weeks — not months — if stakeholders decide quickly.

## Alternative path

If scope is already narrow and risks are low, skip long discovery and start with a short paid sprint that delivers working software.
