If you are a founder or operator hiring someone to build software, the first risk is not technology — it is the delivery model. Many traditional agencies sell a long discovery phase, a fixed quote, and a launch date months away. You pay, wait, and hope.

## What usually goes wrong

- You see decks and mockups for weeks before any production software exists.
- Scope is locked early, so every change becomes a change order.
- Quality (tests, CI/CD, documentation) is optional or billed as extras.
- You cannot tell if the team is stuck until the deadline slips.

## What a better partner looks like

Ask for weekly working deliverables: something you can click, review, and reject. Require open repository access from day one. Prefer flexible sprint pricing over a single inflated fixed quote when requirements are still evolving.

> If you cannot see progress every week, you are not managing a product — you are funding a promise.

## Questions to ask before you hire

- What will I be able to use after the first sprint?
- Who owns the code, credentials, and cloud accounts?
- Are automated tests and CI included by default?
- How do we change priorities without restarting the contract?
