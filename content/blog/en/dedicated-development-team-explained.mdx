Buyers hear “dedicated development team” in almost every sales call. Few get a clear definition. In practice, it means a stable pod allocated to your roadmap for ongoing sprints — not a black-box project with a single end date.

## Dedicated team vs project engagement

- **Project:** fixed (or semi-fixed) scope, defined end, change orders when reality moves.
- **Dedicated team:** capacity you steer weekly; scope evolves with learning.

If your product roadmap will change after the first users, dedicated capacity usually fits better than a giant fixed project.

## When a dedicated team is the right buy

- You have continuous backlog after the MVP.
- You need senior architecture plus delivery speed without hiring full-time yet.
- You want knowledge to stay in one pod instead of restarting with new freelancers every quarter.

## What good dedicated teams include

- Named roles and a lead you can reach.
- Weekly demos and shared backlog.
- Your repository, staging, and cloud from day one.
- Clear ramp-up and ramp-down rules.

## What to watch

- “Dedicated” that is actually shared across five clients with no focus.
- Junior-only staffing after a senior sales demo.
- Contracts that keep IP or environments with the vendor.
