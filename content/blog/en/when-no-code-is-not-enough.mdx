No-code tools help you move before you have engineering capacity. That is valuable. The mistake is staying on them after the product becomes core to revenue, compliance, or customer trust.

## Signs you have outgrown no-code

- Workarounds multiply faster than features.
- You need custom AI (RAG, agents) tied to internal systems.
- Permissions, audit logs, or data residency become requirements.
- Performance or vendor limits block growth.
- Your team spends more time fighting the tool than serving customers.

## How to migrate without a rewrite panic

You do not have to rebuild everything at once. Start with the workflow that creates the most risk or revenue. Keep no-code for peripheral ops while you ship a production core on a real stack (for example Next.js + TypeScript APIs + a proper database).
