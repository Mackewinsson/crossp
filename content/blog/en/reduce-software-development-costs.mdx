Reducing software cost is a real goal. Doing it by removing tests, seniors, and ownership is how companies pay twice.

## Levers that actually lower cost

- **Narrower first slice.** Ship one workflow well instead of five half-built.
- **Faster decisions.** Buyer delay is expensive even when rates look low.
- **Reuse before invent.** Prefer proven stacks over experimental architecture for v1.
- **Visible sprints.** Catch wrong direction in week one, not month six.
- **Keep assets yours.** Rewrites after vendor lock-in destroy any “savings.”

## Fake savings to reject

- Junior-only teams on complex products.
- No staging / no CI “to move faster.”
- Fixed quotes with vague assumptions and change-order traps.
- Offshore rates without documentation or handover.

## A cost-aware buying sequence

1. Ballpark fit on a short call.
2. Clear MVP slice.
3. First sprint with demo.
4. Continue only if progress is real.
