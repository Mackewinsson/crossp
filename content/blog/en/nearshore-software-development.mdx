Nearshore is popular because timezone overlap and cultural proximity reduce friction. It is not magic. Bad process is still bad process closer to your clock.

## Real benefits

- More overlapping hours for demos and decisions.
- Easier live collaboration on complex product work.
- Often better cost/quality balance than purely local senior teams for continuous capacity.

## Risks that still apply

- Vague ownership of code and cloud.
- “Bait and switch” staffing after the sales call.
- No tests, no staging, no senior architecture.

## Evaluate nearshore like any partner

Ask for weekly working software, repository access from day one, named seniors, and a clear commercial model. Location is a bonus — delivery system is the product.
