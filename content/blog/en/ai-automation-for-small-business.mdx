Small businesses are flooded with AI tool pitches. The ones that create ROI share a pattern: they remove repetitive work from people who already know the process.

## High-ROI starting points

- Lead intake and qualification routing.
- Support triage with human handoff.
- Document drafting from structured inputs (quotes, proposals, summaries).
- Internal search over your own policies and product data (with controls).

## Where AI automation disappoints

- Vague “automate the business” projects with no owner.
- Customer-facing bots with no escalation path.
- Automations that touch money or compliance without audit logs.

## Build vs buy for AI automation

Use off-the-shelf tools for simple workflows. Move to custom software when you need proprietary data, permissions, multi-step agents, or integrations your no-code stack cannot hold.

> The goal is not more AI. The goal is fewer hours on work that does not need a human.
