Every operator eventually faces build vs buy. The wrong frame is “custom is always better” or “SaaS is always cheaper.”

## Buy (SaaS) when…

- The workflow is commodity (email, basic CRM, payroll).
- Speed to start matters more than deep differentiation.
- You can accept the vendor’s data model and limits.

## Build custom when…

- The workflow is your competitive edge.
- Off-the-shelf tools force expensive workarounds.
- You need proprietary AI, permissions, or integrations SaaS will not support cleanly.

## The hybrid most companies need

Buy commodity systems. Build the core product or operational engine that makes you unique. Integrate both with clear ownership of credentials and data.

> If a tool is in every competitor’s stack, building it rarely creates advantage.
