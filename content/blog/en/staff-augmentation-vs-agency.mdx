Staff augmentation and agencies both “give you developers.” They solve different problems.

## Staff augmentation in one line

You buy people who work inside your process. You own prioritization, architecture decisions, and management bandwidth.

## Agency / product partner in one line

You buy delivery of a product outcome with a lead who owns execution cadence — ideally with weekly demos and clear ownership of assets.

## Choose staff augmentation when…

- You already have strong engineering leadership.
- Your backlog, tools, and definition of done are mature.
- You only need extra hands, not a delivery system.

## Choose an agency or product partner when…

- You lack senior architecture and cannot wait a hiring cycle.
- You need an MVP or AI feature shipped with accountability.
- You want a partner who challenges bad scope, not just billable hours.

## Hybrid that often works

Use a senior partner to establish architecture and the first production version, then augment or hire once the system is stable.

> If nobody owns architecture, staff augmentation just scales confusion.
