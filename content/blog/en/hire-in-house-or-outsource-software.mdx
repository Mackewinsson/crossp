Business leaders often default to hiring. It feels safer: “our people, our product.” In practice, recruiting senior engineers can take months — and you still do not know if the product fits the market.

## Hire in-house when…

- Software is your core product and you need permanent domain knowledge on payroll.
- You already have engineering leadership and a clear roadmap for 12+ months.
- You can afford a slow ramp while recruiting and onboarding.

## Outsource (or partner) when…

- You need an MVP or AI feature shipped before a fundraising or sales deadline.
- You lack senior architecture and cannot wait a quarter to hire it.
- You want to validate demand before locking in salaries and management overhead.

## The hybrid that works

Many companies use a senior external team to build the first production version, then hire selectively once the product is live and the architecture is stable. That sequence reduces wasted payroll on the wrong stack or wrong scope.
